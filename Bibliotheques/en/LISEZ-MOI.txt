English library (EN) — to produce.

IMPORTANT: the English library is NOT a translation of the French one.
Anglo-saxon reference letters are more direct and less coded. Mimicking
the Swiss-German coded scale in English would produce text that reads
as passive-aggressive to an anglo-saxon recruiter.

Structure to produce:
  - same criteria keys as fr/ for consistency;
  - 3 levels only (positive / neutral / silence) is a reasonable design
    choice, or keep 5 levels but with much more direct language and no
    implicit scale — to be decided with an HR expert.
  - default behavior: explicit positive signal OR silence. Never
    faint-praise constructions.

The blacklist EN should focus on:
  - inadvertent translations of FR/DE coded phrases
  - defamatory phrasings that US/UK case law treats differently from CO
    (reference letter privilege exists but is limited).

Consult an English-language HR/employment law specialist before
producing the content file.
